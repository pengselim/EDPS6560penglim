The History of our People

Hello. My name is Peng Se Lim. I am a Cambodian American. My family immigrated to this country in 1980 as Refugees of the Khmer Rouge . Our story is one of millions. One of hope, of faith, of death and of life. This is the story of how my family came to this country to escape the terrors of war and the Khmer Rouge. The Khmer Rouge killed nearly two million Cambodians from 1975 to 1979, spreading like a virus from the jungles until they controlled the entire country, only to systematically dismantle and destroy it in the name of a Communist agrarian ideal.


The legacy of my family is similar to the video above. During the Khmer Rouge Genocide, my family escaped the most destitute environments to bring our family here to the United States. Over the past 40 years, we have carved out our own path and our own story. The blood that flows in our veins is one that is founded upon a triumph over the most insurmountable odds. The goal of this website is to share this story and others like it with the rest of the world so that the next generation of Khmer/Cambodian Americans will never forget the legacy of Cambodia.
